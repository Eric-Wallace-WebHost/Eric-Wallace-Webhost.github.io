I am a student at the University of Maryland working at the intersection of Deep Learning and Natural Language Processing. I am advised by Jordan Boyd-Graber and a member of the Computational Linguistics and Information Processing Lab.

My research interest is in learning representations from raw data (text, video, audio) to solve problems in Language Understanding and Robotics. At the moment that means using neural networks to extract distributed representations, and training them using Maximum Likelihood and Reinforcement Learning.

Previously I conducted research in GPU Computing and Computational Aerodynamics at the Alfred Gessow Rotorcraft Center underneath Ananth Sridharan and Inderjit Chopra. I was an intern at Intel in Fall 2017.

active projects

Robustness in Deep Natural Language Processing
Current: Shi Feng, Pedro Rodriguez, Eric Wallace, Mohit Iyyer, Jordan Boyd-Graber, and others
Natural Language Processing models that are deployed "in the wild" often face data distributions unlike those seen during training. We are currently exploring ideas in producing and defending against aversarial examples, adapting to domain shift, handling rare words, and normalizing text in an end-to-end manner.

Quiz Bowl: Trivia Based Question Answering
Current: Shi Feng, Pedro Rodriguez, Eric Wallace, Jordan Boyd-Graber, and others
We develop computerized systems to solve Question Answering tasks such as Quiz Bowl. Our system has beaten many human players, including a match against Ken Jennings. We are currently working on developing better Question Answering models using Deep Learning and better buzzing models using Reinforcement Learning.
